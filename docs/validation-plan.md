# Khidr — 4-Week Editorial Validation

The build is cheap; the moat (sustainable, trusted curation) is the risk. Prove you
can do the editorial work **by hand** before investing in heavy product. If you
can't sustain 3–5 good items/week for a month, the app won't save it — and if you
can, you'll launch with content and an audience already warm.

## Goal

Answer two questions:
1. **Can you sustain the curation?** (your time, your judgment, week over week)
2. **Does the action layer resonate?** (do people actually want to call/email off
   the back of a blurb?)

## Method: a manual "Khidr zero" newsletter

Run the product as a plain email/Substack/WhatsApp broadcast — no app.

- **Cadence:** 3–5 items/week, on a fixed day. Consistency > volume.
- **Each item:** headline + your original blurb (use `curation-guide.md` template)
  + link to original + a manual "action" line: the rep-lookup link (e.g.
  5calls/house.gov/senate.gov) and 2–3 neutral talking points.
- **Track per item:** opens, link clicks, and replies/"I called" responses.

## Weekly checklist

- [ ] 3–5 items curated, blurbs original, sources named & linked
- [ ] Each item has neutral talking points (points-to-raise, not demands)
- [ ] Sent on the fixed day
- [ ] Metrics logged (see below)
- [ ] One sentence of self-review: was this sustainable this week? honestly?

## Metrics to log (simple sheet)

| Week | Items | Subscribers | Open rate | Click rate | "I acted" replies | Hours spent |
|------|-------|-------------|-----------|------------|-------------------|-------------|

## Success signals (go/no-go after 4 weeks)

- **Green (build):** you sustained cadence without dread; open rate >35%; a
  meaningful fraction click through and some report acting; **and** time-cost stayed
  under the ceiling below.
- **Amber (adjust):** content good but cadence painful → narrow scope/topics, or
  line up a co-editor before building.
- **Red (rethink):** you couldn't keep cadence, or no one engages with the action
  ask → the moat isn't there yet; fix that before any app spend.

### Hours-per-week is a hard kill criterion, not a logged column

Set an explicit ceiling: **~5 hours/week.** If any week costs materially more (say
9+ hours), that is a **Red on its own, even if every engagement metric is Green.**
The real failure mode here is not "no audience"; it is **Katije getting starved**
because Khidr quietly ate the week. Time-cost is a first-class go/no-go, weighted
equal to engagement. Track it in the sheet and honour it.

## Why this order

The app's action layer (email/WriteToThem-first, personal-not-blast) is already
scaffolded in `mobile/`. It will still be there in 4 weeks. What you can't fake is
the editorial muscle and an audience that trusts it — build that first, plug it into
the app second.
