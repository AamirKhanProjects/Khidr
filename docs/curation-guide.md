# Khidr — Curation & Blurb Guide

The curation is the moat. It is also the main legal/reputational surface. This is
the discipline.

## Copyright: stay an aggregator, not a republisher

**Safe formula per item:** *source headline (or our reworded one) + our own
original blurb + a link that opens the original.*

Do:
- Write the blurb **in our own words**. Summarise the facts; add our POV/framing.
- Keep any directly quoted phrase to a short sentence at most, clearly attributed.
- Always send the user to the original via the in-app browser (drives traffic to
  the publisher — this is what keeps aggregators safe).
- Attribute the source by name on the card.

Don't:
- Paste article body text, multiple paragraphs, or paywalled content.
- Rely on wire services (AP / Reuters / AFP) as primary sources — they are
  aggressive even about headlines. Use them only to *find* a story, then link to a
  publisher that ran it, or write a fully original summary.
- Embed the source's images/video without permission.

## Blurb style (the POV without becoming a political actor)

A blurb should make a busy reader understand **what happened, why it affects them,
and that they have a representative who can act** — without telling them what to
demand. Inform, don't instruct (see `legal-notes.md`).

- **80–140 words.** One screen. No fluff.
- **Lead with the fact**, then the stake. ("The House passed X. It would do Y to Z.")
- **POV = framing and selection**, not commands. "This matters because…" is fine.
  "Call and demand they vote no" is not — that crosses from platform to actor.
- Plain, calm, literate. No outrage-bait, no all-caps, no scare quotes.
- Cite numbers/dates precisely. If unsure, leave it out.

### Template
```
[What happened, 1 sentence, concrete.]
[Why it affects this community, 1–2 sentences, factual stakes.]
[Where it stands / what's next procedurally, 1 sentence.]
— Source: [Publisher]
```

## Action context (powers the call script & AI email draft)

For each item, optionally provide:
- `callPoints`: 2–4 neutral talking points a constituent might raise ("the bill's
  effect on X", "the timeline", "ask where the member stands"). Phrased as *points
  to raise*, not scripted demands.
- `emailContext`: 1–2 factual sentences the AI draft can ground itself in, so the
  user's personal email is accurate.

## Sourcing checklist (per item)

- [ ] Is it genuinely a Muslim-affecting story (policy, civil rights, foreign
      policy, community safety, representation)? Not just tangentially "Muslim".
- [ ] Is the source reputable and the claim verifiable? Two sources for anything
      contested.
- [ ] Blurb written from scratch, no copied body text.
- [ ] Source named + link set.
- [ ] POV present but informational, not a directive.
- [ ] Talking points (if any) are neutral points-to-raise.

## Cadence

Start small and sustainable (see `validation-plan.md`): **3–5 items/week** done
well beats a daily firehose you can't maintain. The moat is trust; one sloppy or
slanted item costs more than ten good ones earn.
